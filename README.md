### <ins>What is Sim Swapping (duh)</ins>
aka Sim Hijacking. It's not new. Attackers recon you via phishing or social media, contact your mobile carrier into transferring your phone num to a new sim card that THEY bought, then cotrol all your shit. They recieve your calls, texts, etc, and can pwn your accounts by resetting pw. How do you know if you've been pwned this way?
You lost cell service (and you KNOW the bill is paid up, ect), and are logged out of your major accounts. 
  How to prevent this:
  - Avoid using two-factor auth, use an app-based authenticator (Google, Microsoft, 2FAS)
  - Set up a PIN on your mobile account

### <ins>Help Desk Exploitation</ins>
Attackers pretend to be helpdesk and get into your shit. 
  How to mitigate this:
  - Least Priviledge
  - Avoid email or sms auth, instead use FIDO2
